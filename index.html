Nelson Mandela

The man who transformed a nation and inspired the world.

This is the timeline of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in Mvezo, South Africa, as Rolihlahla Mandela.

  • 1939 - Enrolls at the University of Fort Hare, where he becomes involved in student politics.

  • 1941 - Joins the African National Congress (ANC) and begins his political activism against apartheid.

  • 1944 - Helps form the ANC Youth League and becomes its first Secretary.

  • 1948 - The National Party implements apartheid, institutionalizing racial segregation in South Africa.

  • 1952 - Participates in the Defiance Campaign against unjust laws, leading to his arrest and conviction.

  • 1961 - Becomes the leader of Umkhonto we Sizwe, the armed wing of the ANC, advocating for sabotage against the apartheid regime.

  • 1962 - Arrested and sentenced to life imprisonment for sabotage and conspiracy to overthrow the government.

  • 1964 - Delivers his famous "I am prepared to die" speech during the Rivonia Trial.

  • 1990 - Released from prison after 27 years, becoming a global symbol of resistance to apartheid.

  • 1994 - Elected as South Africa's first Black president in the country’s first multiracial elections.

  • 1999 - Steps down after one term but continues to work on peace and human rights issues globally.

  • 2013 - Passes away at the age of 95.

"For to be free is not merely to cast off one’s chains, but to live in a way that respects and enhances the freedom of others."

-Nelson Mandela
